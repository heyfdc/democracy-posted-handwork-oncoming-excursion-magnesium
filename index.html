Need to Know

Mt. Fuji - Yoshida Trail Rules (Aug 19) ▾
  • First shuttle from Fujisan Parking: 4:00 AM, arrives 5th Station 4:45 AM. Arrive parking by 3:45 AM to queue.
  • Gate closes 2 PM for all climbers without mountain hut reservations. 4:45 AM start gives you a wide margin.
  • Rangers conduct mandatory gear checks. No sneakers or sandals - proper hiking boots required for all four.
  • Trail capped at 4,000 climbers/day. 1,000 slots released daily at midnight JST. Book at fujisan-climb.jp the moment the 2026 season opens (May/June).
  • Summit temperature ~5°C in August. Bring layers, headlamps, snacks, plenty of water.
  • Gate fee ¥4,000/person - can pre-pay online with reservation or pay cash at gate.
Mt. Fuji - Packing List (gear check is enforced) ▾
  • Mandatory: proper hiking boots (rangers will turn you around), headlamp + spare batteries, warm layers (summit ~5°C even in August), rain jacket
  • Strongly recommended: trekking poles (rental at 5th Station ~¥500), gloves, buff/neck gaiter, snacks with sugar (onigiri, energy gels, chocolate), 2L water minimum
  • At 5th Station: last chance to rent/buy gear, use a proper toilet, eat before the climb. Don't skip this.
  • Mountain huts sell hot food, drinks, and oxygen cans (~¥1,500). Altitude sickness is real - if someone feels severe nausea or dizziness, descend immediately.
  • Sunrise timing Aug 19: approximately 5:00 AM at the summit. With a 4:45 AM 5th Station start and ~6 hr ascent, you won't catch sunrise at the top. The trail and views are excellent in full daylight.
Driving Leg - France, Shuan, Alvin, Steven ▾
  • Rental: Sixt · 1 car · Aug 17–21 · Shibuya Udagawacho branch · confirm ETC card included
  • Drive on the left. Steering wheel on the right. Speed limit: 100 km/h on expressways, 60 km/h on regular roads.
  • ETC lanes: stay in ETC-marked lanes at toll booths - the card handles it automatically. Do not enter cash-only lanes.
  • IDP: France (rental + Fun2Drive) and Steven (Fun2Drive only) - get at AAA before departure. Alvin and Shuan are passengers.
  • Expressway rest stops (PA/SA) are excellent. Ashigara SA on the Tomei near Gotemba is worth a stop.
  • Costco Zama opens 10 AM weekdays - plan pickup timing accordingly.
  • If in an accident: call 110 (police) and 119 (ambulance). Do not move the car. Contact Sixt emergency line - number will be in the rental docs.
  • Return car Aug 21 morning at Shibuya Udagawacho before settling back into Tokyo. Parking in the city is ¥500–1,000/hr and unnecessary.

Tokyo Essentials ▾
  • Suica IC card: buy at NRT/HND airport on arrival. Tap on/off every train, bus, and most convenience stores and vending machines. Load money at any station kiosk.
  • Cash: Japan is still heavily cash-based. Sushi Dai, Golden Gai bars, many ramen shops are cash only. Convenience store ATMs (7-Eleven, Japan Post) accept foreign cards reliably. Always carry ¥10,000+ on you.
  • Google Maps: works perfectly for transit directions - download Tokyo offline map before leaving wifi.
  • Tipping: never tip in Japan. It can be considered rude. Exceptional service is the standard.
  • Train etiquette: no talking on phone, no eating, speak quietly, priority seats for elderly/pregnant. Queuing is strict - stand at marked spots on the platform.
  • Last trains: Yamanote Line runs until ~12:30–1:00 AM. After that, taxi or LUUP. Taxi app: GO app (English-friendly).
  • JR Pass: not worth it for this itinerary - mostly Tokyo metro + driving, not Shinkansen.
  • Weather Aug: 32–36°C / 90–97°F, high humidity. Start every day early. Stay hydrated. Convenience stores have cold drinks and cold towels.
  • Luggage: main bags stay at Airbnb during drive leg (Aug 17–21). Pack only essentials + hiking gear into a single bag each.
  • Obon: ends Aug 16 - domestic travel is at peak before then. Crowds ease significantly once you hit the road Aug 17.
Golden Gai - Shinjuku ▾
  • 200+ bars across 6 narrow alleys. Each seats 4–10 people max. With 5 of you, you'll often take over the whole bar.
  • Cover charges: ¥500–1,500/person, usually includes a small snack (otoshi). Budget ¥2,000–3,000 per bar including 1–2 drinks.
  • Cash only. Best atmosphere 9 PM–1 AM. Walk all six alleys first to get a feel before committing.
  • Start at Albatross - 3 narrow floors, rooftop terrace, English-speaking staff, no cover. Best warm-up bar for newcomers.
  • Others worth finding: Deathmatch in Hell (horror-themed, Japanese whisky, open until 3 AM) · La Jetée (film-themed, classic cocktails) · The Open Book (literary bar, welcomes all visitors) · Golden Gai red. (coffee shochu, American general store vibe)
  • Some bars are members-only or regulars-only - if a place feels unwelcoming, move on. Plenty that love visitors.
